D09 · The 4-Step Method — Your Query-Writing System

Estimated time: ~1.5 hrs

Track your progress and keep notes below.
Today's GoalLearn the system for building any query from scratch. Apply it every time — it prevents every mistake.
  • Step 1 — What is ONE row? The answer becomes your FROM table. One outlet → CustomerOutlet. One salesman day → RouteHistory. One invoice → Transaction.
  • Step 2 — What columns do I need? List them all. Mark which table each lives in. Split into "on anchor table" vs "needs a JOIN".
  • Step 3 — Trace the path. For each JOIN, draw the chain from anchor to target. More than 4 hops? Check for a shortcut.
  • Step 4 — INNER or LEFT? For each JOIN: can the left-side row exist without a right-side match? YES → LEFT JOIN.
✏️ Apply the 4 Steps on Paper

Before writing any code, apply all 4 steps to this requirement:
"Show each salesman's name, their territory, and how many outlets are on their route."

Step 1 hint — what is ONE row here? A salesman? A route day? Think before coding.
